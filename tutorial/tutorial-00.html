The purpose of Endev is to empower non-programmers and programmers alike to build interactive interfaces that allow you to display and edit data. And to do it fast. That way you can quickly build a demo of your idea to test with the potential users or clients, or build an interface for your personal birthday wish list so that even your grandmother can use it :)

About the tutorial setup

Throughout the tutorial you will be shown examples of code and will be encouraged to play with it as well. The code will be presented in CodePen, an interactive tool for working with snippets of HTML. You can choose to stay inside CodePen and play there. You can freely modify the examples (by selecting Edit on in upper right corner of every example) and click Run to see the modifications. If you want to save your modifications you need to click Fork. You don’t need to even have an account to do that, but if you want to be able to later find your code easily it’s suggested that you do create an account.

At the end of each chapter there are assignments to do on your own (i.e. no solution is provided). These assignments are designed to test your understanding of Endev and give us input on possible improvements. Try to do them do them even if you are not sure you'll be able to make them complete.

All the modifications you make to Endev examples are recored annonymously for research purposes.

Good luck!
